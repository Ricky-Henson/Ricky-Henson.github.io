Pearl Milk Tea (珍珠奶茶)

The world's most famous sweet pearl milk teas, also known as bubble tea (or boba tea), originated in Taiwan. Since you're in the country where it was made, you can choose from a variety of flavors and even an degree of sweetness levels and temperature settings.

Bubble tea is a tea -based drink that originated in Taiwan in the early 1980s. It most commonly consists of tea accompanied by chewy tapioca balls ("boba" or "pearls"), but it can be made with other toppings as well such as grass jelly, aloe vera , red beans, etc. Bubble tea has many varieties and flavors, but the two most popular varieties are black pearl milk tea and green pearl milk tea ("pearl" signifies the tapioca balls at the bottom).

Bubble teas fall under two categories: teas without milk and milk teas. Both varieties come with a choice of black, green, or oolong tea as the base. Milk teas usually include powdered milk or fresh milk, but may also use condensed milk, almond milk, soy milk, or coconut milk.

Tshuah-ping is a shaved ice dessert introduced to Taiwan during Taiwan under Japanese rule, and then spread from Taiwan to Greater China and countries with large regional Overseas Chinese populations such as Malaysia and Singapore. It is especially popular in Taiwan where the dish has a variation called xuehua bing (雪花冰), in which the ice is not made out of water but milk.

Taiwanese Ice Cream (芋冰)

Taro ice cream is a common ice cream product in Taiwan. It has a soft taste, sweet but not greasy. The taro ice creaam in Caohu, Dali District, Taichung City is the birthplace. It is famous because Chiang Ching-kuo went to the "Meifang Taro Ice City" to eat it in the 1980s. And taro ice cream has now spread to Yilan County and Jiaxian District of Kaohsiung City. Eating taro ice cream can also be added with peanut powder, coriander dish, or add chocolate sauce, wrap it in spring roll skin and eat it together, it has a unique flavor.

How to make 芋冰:
1. Steam the taro: Peel the taro and cut it into large pieces, put it in an electric cooker, add 2 cups of water to the outer pot, and steam until the switch jumps.
2. Beating: Take out after steaming, add in ingredient B, and beat with a stirring rod until it is uniform and fine.
3. Cook to thicken: Put on the stove and cook while stirring, cook until thick and slightly boiling.
4. Freeze: Let cool and pour into the ice maker. Put it in the freezer until it freezes.




Peanut roll ice cream, also known as spring roll ice or ice cream cake, is one of the major night market/miaokou delicacies. Peanut malt sugar cubes, and then wrapped in taro-flavored ice cream, peanut candy powder and coriander. It tastes very good, with a strong peanut aroma, and it is a dessert that many people love. With the development of the times, the taste of peanut roll ice cream has gradually diversified, from pineapple's flavor is sour to sweet and match the taste of taro just right.

Frog’s egg drink is one of the famous snacks in Taiwan's night markets. After being cooked, the center will appear white, which looks like frog eggs, so it is named. The earliest frogs lay eggs using Xiaokui brand "Shanfenyuan", but later, because the night market began to use "fanyuan" as the material, the taste was accepted by the public, and the public gradually believed that frogs lay eggs using powder balls. Frog laying stalls at Shilin Night Market usually add sugar water or add some creamer to drink it, while other places also add it to shaved ice as a topping. Frog’s egg drink contains small tapioca pearls with hard white centers, resembling frog’s eggs.

Frog’s egg drink is originated from vendors in Shilin Night Market, Wangji Frog Lay Eggs at the entrance of Lane 101, Wenlin Road, Shilin District, Taipei City and Lane 114, Wenlin Road, Shilin District, Taipei City (diagonally opposite to Yangming Theater) )'s Wuji Frog Lay Egg is the earliest founding old shop. At present, almost 90% of the frog lay eggs in the night market are all wrapped up by Wang Ji and Wu Ji.

The two are often sold from the same stall.

Tofu Pudding (豆花)

Douhua means “flower of the soybean” or “bean flower”. It is a Chinese sweet or savoury snack made with very tender tofu. It. This dish may be spicy, savory, or sweet, and eaten at any time of day. Douhua is made with soy milk and a coagulant to help it congeal. Different from making tofu, where you use a coagulant to make the soy milk curdle and then press it, making douhua is a simpler process. You simply need to dissolve the coagulant into the soy milk and let it set. That’s all.




Lei cha or ground tea is a traditional Southern Chinese tea-based beverage or gruel that forms a part of Hakka cuisine. In English, the dish is sometimes called thunder tea since "thunder" (雷; léi) is homonymous with "pounded".

This tea is a warm (or sometimes iced) drink made by pounding tealeaves and a variety of roasted nuts, grains, seeds, herbs, and seasonings, adding hot water, and topping with popped rice. It is often served with mochi.

About Taiwan Night Markets

Taiwan night markets are street markets that open from the evenings until midnight. They can be found across all areas in Taiwan—on old streets, around colleges, or in popular tourist areas.
They are mostly food or drink stalls. Night markets are great for families with kids, couples, friends, or any hungry person looking for a cheap and tasty meal!
